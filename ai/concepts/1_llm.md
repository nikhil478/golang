A large language model is a neural network trained on a vast amount of text for natural language processing tasks, especially language generation. 

LLMs can generate, summarize, translate and parse text in many contexts, and are a foundational technology behind modern chatbots.

- Biased or inaccurate training data can make an LLM's output less reliable

As of 2024, the largest and most capable LLMs are all based on transformer architectures,[3] which, according to the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need", can be more efficient and parallelizable than earlier statistical and recurrent neural network models.

[4] Research into other architectures, such as state space models, is ongoing.[5]

Benchmark evaluations for LLMs attempt to measure model reasoning, factual accuracy, alignment, and safety.[6]
